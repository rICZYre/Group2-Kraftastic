DIY Choker Cutout T-Shirt

by: Anne A. Hollabaugh

Choker Cutout T-Shirt effortlessly blends modern style with DIY creativity. Crafted from a soft and comfortable cotton blend, this t-shirt features a unique choker neckline with a daring cutout detail. The choker neckline adds a touch of sophistication, while the cutout detail lends an element of intrigue and allure.

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Plain t-shirt
  • Fabric marker or chalk
  • Scissors
  • Ruler or measuring tape
  • Iron (optional)
  • Sewing machine or needle and thread (optional, for finishing edges)

Step 1: Choose Your T-Shirt

Select a plain t-shirt in a color and fabric of your choice. Opt for a crew neck or scoop neck style for the best results.

Step 2: Plan Your Design

Decide where you want the choker cutout to be on your t-shirt. The choker can be centered or off-center, depending on your preference. Use a fabric marker or chalk to lightly mark the desired area on the neckline of the t-shirt.

Step 3: Measure and Mark the Cutout

Measure the width and depth of the cutout you want to create for the choker. Use a ruler or measuring tape to ensure accuracy. Mark the measurements onto the neckline of the t-shirt using a fabric marker or chalk.

Step 4: Cut Out the Choker

Carefully cut along the marked lines to create the choker cutout. Start by making a small slit at the center of the neckline, then cut outwards along the marked lines. Take your time to ensure smooth and even cuts.

Step 5: Optional: Finish the Edges

If desired, you can finish the edges of the cutout to prevent fraying and give the choker a polished look. You can use a sewing machine to sew a narrow hem around the edge of the cutout, or use a needle and thread to hand-sew a blanket stitch or whipstitch along the edge.

Step 6: Optional: Press the Choker

If the fabric of your t-shirt tends to curl at the edges after cutting, you can use an iron to press the choker flat. Place a pressing cloth or scrap piece of fabric over the choker area to protect it, then gently press with a warm iron.

Step 7: Try on Your DIY Choker Cutout T-Shirt

Once you've finished creating the choker cutout, try on your t-shirt to see how it looks and fits. Adjust the cutout if necessary to achieve your desired style.

Step 8: Style and Wear Your T-Shirt

Now that your DIY choker cutout t-shirt is complete, style it with your favorite bottoms and accessories for a trendy and unique look. Pair it with jeans, skirts, or shorts for casual outings, or dress it up with statement jewelry for a night out.

Step 9: Show Off Your Creation!

Rock your DIY choker cutout t-shirt with confidence and pride. Show off your creativity and style by wearing your handmade creation wherever you go!
