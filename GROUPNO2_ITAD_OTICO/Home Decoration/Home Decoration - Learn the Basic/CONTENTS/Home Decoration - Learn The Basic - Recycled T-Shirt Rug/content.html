Recycled T-Shirt Rug

by: instructables

A DIY recycled T-shirt rug is a handmade rug created from old T-shirts that have been repurposed into a new functional item. Instead of throwing away old clothing, this project involves cutting the T-shirts into strips, braiding or otherwise weaving them together, and forming them into a rug. This eco-friendly DIY project not only reduces waste by giving new life to old materials but also provides an opportunity for creativity and personalization as you choose the colors and patterns of the T-shirts used in the rug.

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • flat doormat
  • 5-8 old T-shirts
  • scissors
  • hot glue gun and hot glue

STEP 1: Cut T-Shirt Strips

Starting at the bottom hem, cut T-shirts across into 2-inch strips. Then cut the 2-inch strips into 4-5-inch long sections. 

STEP 2: Tie T-Shirt Knots

Tie a knot into the center of each T-shirt section. Note: Prepare all the knotted pieces before you start gluing.

STEP 3: Add First Row of Knots to Rug

Place a strip of hot glue along one side of the doormat. Stick each knot into the glue . Continue the strip of glue and add more knotted pieces. Glue the knots with 1/4 inch between them . Tip: If you're using a variety of colored T-shirts, glue the colors in a random pattern. It'll create a nice multicolored effect on the doormat.

STEP 4: Continue Gluing Knots to Mat

Keep gluing T-shirt sections in one straight line along an edge of the mat . Continue to glue knotted T-shirt sections all the way up the edge of the mat. Move onto the next row. Place a strip of hot glue 1 inch from the previous row. Stick the knots into the glue strip, leaving 1/2 inch between each knot. Continue to add glue and knotted sections until the full doormat is covered.

You're Finished!
Use your personalized doormat to wipe off your shoes when you walk in from class.
