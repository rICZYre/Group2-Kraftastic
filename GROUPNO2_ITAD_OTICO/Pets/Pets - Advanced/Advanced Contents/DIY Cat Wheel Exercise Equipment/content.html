DIY Cat Wheel Exercise Equipment

by: Katja Hansen

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Wooden board or plywood (for the wheel frame)
  • PVC pipe or wooden dowels (for the wheel axles)
  • PVC connectors or wooden blocks (for securing the axles)
  • Rollerblade or skateboard wheels (for smooth rotation)
  • Nuts, bolts, and washers
  • Non-toxic paint or stain (optional)
  • Carpet or grip tape (for traction)
  • Drill and drill bits
  • Saw
  • Sandpaper
  • Measuring tape
  • Pencil

A DIY cat wheel exercise equipment is a fantastic way to provide your feline friend with physical exercise and mental stimulation. Cat wheels, also known as cat exercise wheels or running wheels, allow cats to engage in natural running and chasing behaviors indoors. By creating your own cat wheel, you can tailor it to suit your cat's size and preferences while providing a safe and enriching environment for them to stay active.

Step 1: Design the Wheel Frame

Design the Wheel Frame Determine the size and shape of the cat wheel based on your cat's size and available space. Sketch out a design for the wheel frame on the wooden board or plywood, ensuring it's wide enough for your cat to comfortably run on.

Step 2: Cut the Wooden Board

Cut the Wooden Board Use a saw to cut the wooden board or plywood into the desired shape for the wheel frame. Sand down any rough edges to prevent splinters.

Step 3: Cut the Axles Measure

Cut the Axles Measure and cut the PVC pipe or wooden dowels to the appropriate length for the wheel axles. These should be long enough to extend beyond the width of the wheel frame on both sides.

Step 4: Attach the Axles Drill

Attach the Axles Drill holes in the wheel frame where the axles will be attached. Insert the axles through the holes and secure them in place using PVC connectors or wooden blocks. Use nuts, bolts, and washers to ensure they are tightly fastened.

Step 5: Attach the Wheels

Attach the Wheels Slide the rollerblade or skateboard wheels onto the axles and secure them in place with nuts and bolts. Make sure the wheels are positioned evenly and rotate smoothly.

Step 6: Paint or Stain (Optional)

Paint or Stain (Optional) If desired, paint or stain the wheel frame to match your home decor or add visual interest. Allow the paint or stain to dry completely before proceeding.

Step 7: Add Traction Cover

Add Traction Cover the surface of the wheel with carpet or grip tape to provide traction for your cat's paws. This will prevent slipping and ensure a comfortable running surface.

Step 8: Test the Wheel

Test the Wheel Once the cat wheel is assembled, place it on the ground and encourage your cat to interact with it. Use treats or toys to entice them to walk or run on the wheel, and provide praise and rewards for positive behavior.
