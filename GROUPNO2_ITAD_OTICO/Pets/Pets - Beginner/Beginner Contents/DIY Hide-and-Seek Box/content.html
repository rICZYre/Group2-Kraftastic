DIY Hide-and- Seek Box

by: Marion Kane

If you're like me, and thousands of others who have a nondescript black suitcase that is always a trick to pick out of a conveyor belt crowd, this leather luggage tag Instructable is for you!.

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Cardboard box (medium to large size)
  • Scissors or a utility knife
  • Non-toxic glue or tape
  • Small toys or treats for hiding
  • Optional: decorative paper, paint, or markers for embellishment

consists of a box with various holes and compartments where you can hide treats or toys for your cat to discover. This interactive game taps into your cat's natural hunting instincts and encourages them to explore, sniff, and paw at different openings to uncover hidden treasures. With a few basic materials and a little creativity, you can create a fun and entertaining game that will keep your cat entertained for hours.

Step 1: Select a Suitable Box

Select a Suitable Box Choose a cardboard box that is large enough for your cat to comfortably enter and explore but not too big that it takes up too much space in your home. A medium to large-sized box works well for this project.

Step 2: Plan Your Design

Plan Your Design Decide where you want to create openings and compartments in the box for hiding treats or toys. You can cut holes of various sizes and shapes on different sides of the box to add complexity to the game.

Step 3: Cut Openings

Cut Openings Use scissors or a utility knife to carefully cut openings in the box according to your planned design. Make sure the edges are smooth to prevent any sharp edges that could harm your cat.

Step 4: Create Compartments

Create Compartments Inside the box, you can create compartments or dividers using additional pieces of cardboard. These compartments will help hide treats or toys and add more challenge to the game.

Step 5: Secure the Box

Secure the Box Once you've finished cutting openings and creating compartments, use non-toxic glue or tape to secure any loose edges and reinforce the structure of the box. You want it to be sturdy enough to withstand your cat's curiosity and play.

Step 6: Decorate (Optional)

Decorate (Optional) If you'd like, you can decorate the outside of the box with decorative paper, paint, or markers to make it more visually appealing. Get creative and personalize the box to match your cat's personality or your home decor.

Step 7: Hide Treats or Toys Place

Hide Treats or Toys Place small toys or treats inside the compartments and openings of the box. Start with easy-to-find locations at first and gradually increase the difficulty as your cat becomes more proficient at the game.

Step 8: Introduce to Your Cat

Introduce to Your Cat Show your cat the hide-and-seek box and encourage them to explore it on their own. You can sprinkle some catnip or use their favorite toys to entice them to investigate. Be patient and let them discover the hidden treasures at their own pace.
