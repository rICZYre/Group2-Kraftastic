DIY Dog Treat Dispenser Machine

by: Beth Terry



MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • Cardboard box or plastic container (with lid)
  • PVC pipes or cardboard tubes
  • Non-toxic paint or markers for decoration
  • Scissors or a utility knife
  • Hot glue gun and glue sticks
  • Wooden dowels or skewers
  • Treats or kibble for filling the dispenser
  • Optional: small bells or noise-making devices

A DIY dog treat dispenser machine is a fun and interactive way to engage your canine companion while providing mental stimulation and rewarding them with treats. These machines challenge dogs to problem-solve and work for their rewards, keeping them entertained and mentally sharp. By creating your own treat dispenser machine, you can customize it to fit your dog's size and preferences, while also saving money compared to store-bought options.

Step 1: Choose Your Container

Choose Your Container Select a sturdy cardboard box or plastic container with a lid as the base for your treat dispenser machine. Ensure it's large enough to hold the treats and has a lid that can be opened and closed easily.

Step 2: Decorate the Container

Decorate the Container Use non-toxic paint or markers to decorate the outside of the container with fun designs and patterns. Get creative and personalize it to match your dog's personality or your home decor.

Step 3: Create the Dispensing Mechanism

Create the Dispensing Mechanism Cut holes in the lid of the container large enough to fit PVC pipes or cardboard tubes. These will serve as the dispensing channels for the treats. Arrange them in a grid pattern or randomly across the lid.

Step 4: Attach the Dispensing Channels

Attach the Dispensing Channels Use a hot glue gun to attach the PVC pipes or cardboard tubes to the lid of the container, ensuring they are securely in place. Make sure the openings of the pipes or tubes are facing downwards.

Step 5: Add Obstacles (Optional)

Add Obstacles (Optional) To increase the challenge and difficulty level, you can add obstacles inside the container that the treats must navigate around or through to reach the dispensing channels. Use wooden dowels or skewers to create barriers or tunnels.

Step 6: Fill with Treats

Fill with Treats Once the dispenser is assembled, fill the container with treats or kibble. Make sure to use small, bite-sized treats that can easily fit through the dispensing channels.

Step 7: Optional: Add Close the Lid

Close the Lid Close the lid of the container and secure it in place. Make sure it's sealed tightly to prevent your dog from accessing the treats without using the dispenser.
