DIY Rabbit Hay Feeder with PVC Pipe

by: Anna Hasselbring

MATERIALS NEEDED:

  • PVC pipe (diameter and length depending on the size of your rabbit and the amount of hay needed)
  • PVC pipe end caps (2)
  • PVC pipe elbows (2)
  • PVC pipe T-joint (1)
  • PVC pipe adhesive (optional)
  • Saw or PVC pipe cutter
  • Sandpaper (optional)

A DIY rabbit hay feeder with PVC pipe is a practical and efficient way to provide your bunny with fresh hay while minimizing waste and mess. Rabbits need a constant supply of hay for their dental health and digestion, and a hay feeder helps keep the hay clean and accessible. Using PVC pipe for the feeder ensures durability and allows for easy cleaning. This project is simple to make and can be customized to fit your rabbit's cage or enclosure.

STEP 1: Measure and Cut the PVC

Measure and Cut the PVC Pipe Determine the desired length of the hay feeder based on the size of your rabbit and the available space in their enclosure. Use a saw or PVC pipe cutter to cut the PVC pipe to the desired length. 

STEP 2: Prepare the Pipe

Prepare the Pipe Ends If the edges of the PVC pipe are rough, use sandpaper to smooth them down. This will prevent your rabbit from getting scratched or injured.

STEP 3: Attach the End

Attach the End CapsPlace a PVC pipe end cap on each end of the PVC pipe. If necessary, use PVC pipe adhesive to secure the end caps in place. This will prevent hay from falling out of the ends of the feeder.

STEP 4: Attach the Elbows

Attach the Elbows Attach a PVC pipe elbow to each end of the PVC pipe. These elbows will allow the feeder to be mounted vertically in your rabbit's enclosure.

STEP 5: Attach the T-joint

Attach the T-joint Attach a PVC pipe T-joint to the middle of the PVC pipe. This will serve as the opening where your rabbit can access the hay.

STEP 6: Install the Feeder Mount

Install the Feeder Mount the hay feeder vertically in your rabbit's enclosure, securing it to the cage bars or walls using zip ties or wire. Make sure it is positioned at a comfortable height for your rabbit to reach.

STEP 7: Fill with Hay Fill

Fill with Hay Fill the PVC pipe with hay, stuffing it through the opening in the T-joint. The hay will be held securely inside the feeder, allowing your rabbit to pull out strands as needed.

STEP 8: Monitor and Refill

Monitor and Refill Check the hay feeder regularly to ensure it stays filled with fresh hay. Refill as needed to ensure your rabbit always has access to hay.
